// Step-by-Step Execution Guide
US-friendly exchanges only
The BTC funding rate strategy is the most liquid and lowest-risk version of this trade. You earn funding payments every 8 hours from perpetual futures traders who are paying to stay long BTC. When sentiment is bullish, this rate spikes — sometimes to 0.05–0.1% per 8h (65%+ APY). Your BTC spot position hedges all price exposure. You do not care if BTC goes up or down.
1
Open a Coinbase Advanced account
Coinbase is the best US-regulated spot exchange for this strategy. Complete KYC and fund with USD. Use Coinbase Advanced — fees are 0.4–0.6% vs 1.5%+ on the basic app.
💡 Why Coinbase
FDIC-insured USD, strong regulatory standing, excellent BTC/USD liquidity. Best choice for the spot leg of this trade for US users.
Open Coinbase Account →
2
Open a Kraken account for perp trading
For US users, Kraken Futures is one of the few regulated perp platforms available. Bybit and Binance Futures are off-limits for US residents. Deposit stablecoins (USDC/USDT) as margin — not USD directly.
⚠ US Perp Exchange Reality
Your realistic options: Kraken Futures and dYdX (decentralized). Kraken's fees are higher than offshore but it's fully regulated. dYdX is an alternative for more advanced users.
Open Kraken Account →
3
Confirm the current BTC funding rate
Check the live table above before entering. As a rule of thumb:

Above 0.01%/8h = worth considering (~11% APY gross)
Above 0.03%/8h = attractive (~33% APY gross)
Above 0.05%/8h = excellent (~55% APY gross)
⚠ Rates Change
Funding rates update every 8 hours based on market sentiment. If the rate goes negative, you pay instead of collect. Monitor daily and exit if it flips and stays negative for 2+ intervals.
4
Buy BTC spot on Coinbase Advanced
Use a limit order, not market. Set it 0.01–0.05% below the ask to reduce slippage. Size your position — start small ($1k–$5k) until comfortable. The strategy scales linearly.
💡 Sizing
If deploying $10,000, buy ~0.15 BTC (at current prices). Keep exact BTC amount noted — you need to match it precisely on the perp leg.
5
Open an equal BTC-PERP short on Kraken Futures
Short the exact same BTC amount at 1x leverage. You are not here to speculate — higher leverage adds liquidation risk with zero upside for this strategy. Use a limit order at current market price.
🚨 Match Sizes Exactly
If you bought 0.15 BTC spot, short exactly 0.15 BTC perp. Any mismatch creates directional exposure. Double-check the math before confirming.
6
Monitor and collect
Funding hits your Kraken account at 00:00, 08:00, and 16:00 UTC. Check daily:

• Funding rate still positive?
• Kraken margin ratio healthy?
• Spot and perp prices tracking close?
💡 Exit Order
Close the perp short first, then sell spot. This minimizes the window where you have unhedged exposure. Set a calendar reminder to review every 48 hours.
ETH works identically to BTC but tends to have higher funding rates during alt-season and around Ethereum-specific events. Good second position after running the BTC trade successfully. ETH is slightly more volatile so entry threshold should be higher.
1
Confirm ETH funding rate is attractive
Look for 0.015%+ per 8h as minimum entry for ETH given slightly higher volatility. ETH rates diverge most during Ethereum upgrades, ETF news cycles, and staking yield changes.
2
Buy ETH spot on Coinbase Advanced
Use a limit order. ETH/USD has excellent liquidity. Size in whole ETH amounts for easier math (e.g., 3 ETH, 5 ETH).
3
Short ETH-PERP on Kraken Futures at 1x
Match the ETH amount exactly. 1x leverage only.
⚠ ETH Staking Context
If ETH staking yield (~3.5% APY) rises, it can compress funding rates. Check the staking rate vs funding rate spread — the trade only makes sense if funding significantly exceeds staking yield.
4
Monitor and collect
Same cadence as BTC. ETH may require slightly more active watching — consider price alerts if ETH moves 10%+ intraday to verify your hedge hasn't drifted.
SOL funding rates are often the highest of the major assets during bull markets — sometimes exceeding 0.05%/8h for extended periods. Higher risk, higher reward. Recommended only after running the BTC trade successfully. SOL is more volatile and perp liquidity is thinner.
1
Only enter above 0.02%/8h for SOL
Higher entry threshold (~22% APY gross minimum) to account for wider spreads, higher slippage, and greater volatility risk on both legs.
2
Buy SOL spot on Coinbase or Kraken
Kraken has solid SOL/USD liquidity. Buy in round amounts (50 SOL, 100 SOL) for simpler sizing math on the perp leg.
3
Short SOL-PERP at 1x on Kraken Futures
1x leverage. Keep 50%+ margin cushion above liquidation — SOL's volatility means you need more buffer than with BTC/ETH.
🚨 Higher Risk
SOL can move 15–20% in a day. This can temporarily squeeze perp margin before spot gains catch up. Keep position size conservative (max 5–10% of portfolio) and hold excess margin on Kraken.
4
Monitor twice daily
Check morning and evening at minimum. SOL funding windows are shorter and rates flip more often. Have a clear exit plan defined before you enter the position.
// Risk Factors
🔄
Funding Rate Flip
Rates change every 8 hours. If sentiment shifts bearish, the rate can go negative — you'd pay rather than collect. Exit both legs promptly if this holds for 2+ intervals.
📉
Margin Liquidation
A sharp price drop can squeeze your perp margin before spot gains are realized. Always keep 2x+ required margin and never use more than 1x leverage on the perp leg.
🏦
Exchange Risk
Coinbase spot is as safe as regulated crypto gets. The perp leg on Kraken carries counterparty risk. Never keep more on any exchange than you're prepared to lose entirely.
